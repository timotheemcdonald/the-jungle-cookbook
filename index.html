Ingredients:

Cookies

2 cups flour

1/2 teaspoon baking powder

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 tablespoon cornstarch

1 cup granulated sugar

2 ripe bananas

2 tablespoons water

1 teaspoon Banana extract (Optional)

Yellow food coloring (Optional)

Frosting

4 cups powdered sugar

1 cup coconut oil

1 cup shredded coconut

1 tablespoon corn syrup

1/2 teaspoon Vanilla extract (Optional)

1/2 teaspoon Banana extract (Optional)

Directions:

1.

In a large bowl with eletric hand-mixer cream together granulated sugar, coconut oil, and bananas. In a small bowl, mix cornstarch and water. Add to the creamed mixture. Add flavoring, if usingg. For bright yellow cookies, add food coloring.

2.

In a medium bowl gently spoon in flour. Add baking powder and salt. Combine gently with a fork or butter knife.

3.

Add the dry ingredients to the creamed mixture in three batches until just mixed. Do not overmix.

4.

Form dough into two equal-sized discs and wrap in plastic wrap. Chill in the refrigerator for two hours or overnight.

5.

Preheat Oven to 325 degrees. Take out chilled dough and roll into a flat rectangle 1/2 inch thick. Using a Drinking glass, cut into circular shapes. Spray baking sheet with cooking spray and place cookies on sheet in four rows of three.

6.

Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Cookies may be soft. Let sit on baking sheet for 3 minutes before transferring to a wire sheet to cool.

7.

Make the Frosting: cream together powdered sugar and coconut oil with electric hand-mixer. Add flavorings if using. Once thoroughly combined, stir in shredded coconut.

8.

Frost cookies once completely cooled, leaving space around the edge. Excess frosting may be stored in the freezer.
